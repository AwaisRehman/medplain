ORIGINAL:
For a 6-year-old with moderate persistent asthma, prescribe fluticasone propionate inhaler 88 mcg, two inhalations twice daily via spacer as a daily controller, and albuterol 90 mcg, two puffs every 4 to 6 hours as needed for wheeze as a rescue inhaler. Emphasize that the fluticasone controller must be taken every day even when well, and is not for sudden symptoms. Rinse the mouth after each fluticasone dose to prevent oral thrush. Return for reassessment in 4 weeks, and seek emergency care if the rescue inhaler is needed more than every 4 hours or speech becomes difficult.

SIMPLIFIED:
For a 6-year-old with moderate persistent asthma, use these medicines:

- Fluticasone propionate inhaler: 2 puffs twice daily via spacer.
- Albuterol inhaler: 2 puffs every 4 to 6 hours as needed for wheeze.

The fluticasone must be taken every day even when the child feels well. It is not for sudden symptoms. After each dose of fluticasone, rinse mouth to prevent a yeast infection in the mouth (oral thrush).

Return to see the doctor in 4 weeks. Go to emergency care if:
- The rescue inhaler is needed more than every 4 hours.
- Speech becomes difficult.
