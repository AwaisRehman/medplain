ORIGINAL:
Initiate concurrent chemoradiotherapy with cisplatin 100 mg/m2 IV on days 1, 22, and 43 alongside definitive radiotherapy 70 Gy in 35 fractions for stage IVA locally advanced squamous cell carcinoma of the oropharynx; ensure adequate hydration and antiemetic prophylaxis before each cisplatin dose, obtain audiometry and renal function at baseline, and place a prophylactic gastrostomy tube prior to treatment to maintain nutrition. Hold cisplatin for creatinine clearance below 50 mL/min and substitute carboplatin AUC 5 if nephrotoxicity develops.

SIMPLIFIED:
Start chemotherapy with radiotherapy for stage IVA throat cancer. Take cisplatin (100 mg/m2 IV) on days 1, 22, and 43 along with 70 Gy of radiation in 35 doses. Drink plenty of water and take anti-nausea medicine before each cisplatin dose. Get hearing and kidney tests done first. Put in a feeding tube to help eat before treatment starts. Stop cisplatin if your creatinine clearance is below 50 mL/min. If kidneys are damaged, use carboplatin (AUC 5) instead.

KNOWN TRAPS TO CHECK BY HAND:
- 'concurrent'/'definitive' chemoradiotherapy carries treatment-intent meaning that must not be dropped.
- Dose is per body surface area (mg/m2), not a flat mg dose — dropping '/m2' changes the meaning.
- 'prophylactic gastrostomy tube' is preventive; must not be reframed as treating an existing problem.
- Substitution to carboplatin is conditional on nephrotoxicity — the condition must survive.
- 'squamous cell carcinoma of the oropharynx' should not be generalized to a different cancer site.
